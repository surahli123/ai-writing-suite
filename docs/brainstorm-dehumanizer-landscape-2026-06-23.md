## TL;DR

The single biggest enhancement is the over-stepping / reader-presumption stance-tell (反代入式越位感): the writer crossing the statement boundary to think FOR the reader, manufacture a misconception, then "correct" it from a superior position. I verified zero analog exists in the catalog (no 越位/反代入/presum entries) or in any of the 9 competing humanizers — it is the suite's one genuinely novel, hard-to-copy asset, and because every individual word is clean, it must live as a catalog entry + write-time self-check + advisory judge dimension, never a regex. The second theme is honest bilingual EN+ZH reach: the source is a 中英文 roundup and the suite documents CJK as its #1 gap, so we can close the catalog/prose side now (a generic ZH AI-vocabulary list + 翻译腔 connective tell, catalog-only) while keeping the stdlib detector explicitly EN-scored and CJK-UNSCORED — no faked Chinese score. The third theme is workflow architecture: the comms-polish/comms-draft flows currently fuse scan-and-remove and lack a compression pass, so adding a classify-before-edit phase, a soft "cut the correct-but-empty sentences" step, a provenance pre-write question, and an explicit "don't over-fix / a clean draft can ship unchanged" stop-gate are all low-risk, high-leverage process edits the article rates as its top moves. Throughout, the detector stays a regression signal — the refusal to ship a 0-100 quality KPI is itself the differentiator.

## 2. Differentiation — why this suite stays distinct

The 9 competitors collapse to one verb — "humanize this text" — and 8 of them ship a self-scored 0-100 number the model then rewrites toward, which is Goodhart's law in action (a piece can score 4/100 and still fabricate facts or read soulless). This suite stays distinct on five axes competitors structurally cannot copy. (1) Honesty of measurement: the detector is a CI regression signal, never a quality KPI; the judge is advisory and never gates CI; the baseline is calibrated to fail 30-40% against human labels (verified hard-asserted at calibration.py:31-32). Refusing the vanity score IS the moat. (2) Engine-vs-fuel: the OSS repo ships only the engine + a generic KB; the company's real Comms Playbook drops in via Confluence and never enters the public repo — competitors' fixed catalogs ARE their product, so they can't offer this split. (3) Stance-level coverage: the over-stepping tell reaches the "thinking" layer the article says matters most and no pattern-matcher can touch. (4) Bilingual reasoning across EN+ZH catalog/playbook/prose (the EN-only and ZH-only competitors each serve half the market), honestly scoped so we never claim a CJK detector score we don't have. (5) Persistent, human-gated voice memory (10-dimension profile with a schema-contract test) vs competitors' transient pasted samples or unsupervised auto-learners. Positioning should lead with the 4-stage workflow (qa→draft→polish→onboard), not "humanizer #10," to avoid a raw-pattern-count fight the suite shouldn't enter.

## 5. Anti-recommendations — explicitly do NOT build

- **Do NOT ship a user-facing 0-100 'AI smell' quality score as a gate or optimization target** — All three reviewers rated this the highest-value decline. It IS constraint 1 stated as a deliberate choice. Every numeric-score competitor (Aboudjem, brandonwise 78→4, stop-slop, op7418, dongbeixiaohuo) has the model score its own output and rewrite until the number drops — Goodhart's law: a piece can score 4/100 and still fabricate facts or read soulless. *Distinctive techniques:*

*Approach:* A curated rule-set (banned phrases + structural clichés + sentence-level checks) applied as a pre-delivery checklist and scored on 5 prose dimensions; the model self-edits against explicit lists before returning any prose.
*Distinctive techniques:*
  - **False Agency detector** — Explicitly names the AI habit of giving inanimate things human verbs — 'the complaint becomes a fix', 'the decision emerges', 'the data tells us' — and mandates naming the human actor instead.   - **Narrator-from-a-Distance rule** — Bans disembodied observation voice ('Nobody designed this', 'People tend to...') and requires putting the reader in the scene. Fix formula: 'You' beats 'People'; 'You don't sit down one day and decide...' beats 'Nobody designed this.'

  - **Em-dash total ban** — Absolute rule, no exceptions: remove every em dash, replace with comma or period. Listed in both Quick Checks and Rhythm Patterns sections as a hard signal.
  - **Three-item list reduction rule** — Flags three-item lists as a rhythmic AI tell. Fix: reduce to two items or one. 'Speed. Quality. Cost.' → 'Speed, quality, cost—pick two.'

  - Empirical indicating-word lexicons: the HC3 chatgpt/human word lists (e.g. 'Certainly!', 'Sure!', 'Let me know if you have any other questions', 'AI language model') derived from real paired corpus statistics — our suite has heuristic rules but not corpus-derived frequency lexicons.
  - QA-pair conditioned detection: question+answer joint model — our detectors are answer-only, missing context-conditioned signal.
  - Bilingual (EN+ZH) detection parity: Chinese indicating-word lists and Chinese RoBERTa detector — our suite is EN-only.
  - Domain-stratified OOD benchmark: held-out domain splits to measure generalization failure — we have no systematic cross-domain regression test.
