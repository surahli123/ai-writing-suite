# Punctuation & Formatting Tells

Surface marks and formatting. Some of these (citation-markup leaks, UTM params,
unfilled placeholders) are *fingerprints* — near-proof the text was pasted from a
chat tool without cleanup — and are worth catching even when nothing else in the
text reads as AI. Others (curly quotes, a lone em dash) are weak signals that
only count in clusters.

---

### F1 — Em / en dashes

- **Tell:** Overuse of em dashes (—) and en dashes (–), mimicking punchy
  sales/editorial rhythm. One of the most reliable single AI formatting tells.
  Catch spaced em dashes ( — ) and double-hyphen substitutes ( -- ) too.
- **Fix:** Replace, in rough order of preference: period (new sentence), comma
  (tight aside), colon (introducing an explanation), parentheses (true aside), or
  restructure. **Calibration:** a lone em dash means nothing — many editors use
  them. It's evidence only when paired with other tells, or above ~1 per 1,000
  words.
- **Sources:** blader (P13), aboudjem (§14), avoid-ai (em dashes), stop-slop.

---

### F2 — Bold overuse / erratic inline bolding

- **Tell:** Two shapes. (a) Mechanical bold on every other phrase or term. (b)
  Patternless bold mid-paragraph — the model decided some words felt important
  with no consistent rule.
- **Fix:** Strip inline bold except glossary terms and UI labels. One bolded
  phrase per major section at most. If something deserves emphasis, sentence
  structure should provide it.
- **Sources:** blader (P14 + P42), aboudjem (§15), avoid-ai (bold overuse),
  anti-vibe (decorative bolding).

---

### F3 — Emoji / icon dressing in headers

- **Tell:** Decorative emoji on headings or bullets: "🚀 Launch Phase," "💡 Key
  Insight," "✨ Key Features." Adds template feel, not energy.
- **Fix:** Remove. **Exception:** social posts may use one or two sparingly, at
  end of line, never mid-sentence.
- **Sources:** aboudjem (§18), avoid-ai (emoji in headers), anti-vibe (emoji /
  icon dressing).

---

### F4 — Curly quotes & typographic signatures

- **Tell:** ChatGPT emits curly quotes (" " ' ') where the target context would
  use straight ones; consistent Oxford commas.
- **Fix:** Replace with straight quotes **only in plain-text / code contexts**
  (code comments, commit messages, plaintext drafts) where nothing auto-curls.
  **Calibration — weak signal:** Word, Google Docs, macOS, iOS all auto-curl, so
  most human prose has curly quotes. Never conclusive alone; don't flag curly
  apostrophes on their own. Leave them in finished publications and locale-correct
  punctuation (« », „ ").
- **Sources:** blader (P17), aboudjem (§19), avoid-ai (curly quotes).

---

### F5 — Title case in headings

- **Tell:** Capitalizing all main words: "Strategic Negotiations And Global
  Partnerships."
- **Fix:** Sentence case for subheadings: "Strategic negotiations and global
  partnerships." Title case only for the piece's main title, if at all.
- **Sources:** blader (P16), aboudjem (§17), avoid-ai (title case headings).

---

### F6 — Markdown bleeding

- **Tell:** Markdown syntax appearing where it won't render: `**bold**` in emails,
  social posts, or Word docs.
- **Fix:** Strip the markup for the target medium.
- **Sources:** blader (P28).

---

### F7 — Hashtag stuffing

- **Tell:** Long trailing hashtag blocks (6+ on a short post) mixing a project
  tag with broad category tags (#AI #Crypto #Web3 #Innovation #FutureTech). Near-
  universal in LLM social content, rare in thoughtful human posts.
- **Fix:** 2-3 specific tags max, or none. If a tag wouldn't help a reader find
  related work, it's filler. (Threshold: 6+ is a hard flag; 5+ a soft tell on
  LinkedIn / investor profiles.)
- **Sources:** avoid-ai (hashtag stuffing).

---

### F8 — Unfilled placeholders

- **Tell:** Bracketed slot-fillers shipped without replacement: `[Your Name]`,
  `[INSERT SOURCE URL]`, `[Describe the specific section]`, `2025-XX-XX`,
  `<!-- Add citation if available -->`. Near-definitive evidence of pasted
  boilerplate.
- **Fix:** Fill with real content or delete the sentence. Treat any visible
  placeholder as a publishing bug.
- **Sources:** blader (P33), avoid-ai (unfilled placeholders).

---

### F9 — Chatbot citation-markup leaks (fingerprint)

- **Tell:** Internal citation tokens that survive copy-paste from chat UIs:
  `citeturn0search0`, `contentReference[oaicite:0]{index=0}`, `oai_citation`,
  `[attached_file:1]`, `grok_card`. Not a pattern — a fingerprint. Essentially
  proof the text was generated by a specific tool and pasted without cleanup.
- **Fix:** Strip every markup token. If a citation was meaningful, replace with a
  real reference. Don't humanize the markup — delete it. Worth catching even when
  nothing else reads as AI.
- **Sources:** blader (P34), avoid-ai (citation-markup leaks).

---

### F10 — AI-tool URL parameters (fingerprint)

- **Tell:** Tracking params AI tools auto-append to generated URLs:
  `utm_source=chatgpt.com`, `utm_source=copilot.com`, `utm_source=openai`,
  `utm_source=claude.ai`, `utm_source=perplexity.ai`, `referrer=grok.com`. The
  parameter is the signature regardless of surrounding text.
- **Fix:** Strip the parameter from every URL; keep the URL if the link is
  meaningful.
- **Sources:** blader (P35), avoid-ai (AI-tool URL parameters).
