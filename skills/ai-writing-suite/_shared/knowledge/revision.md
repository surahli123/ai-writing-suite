# Revision — the draft is raw material; editing makes it good

> **Generic best-practice entry.** Distilled from the four MIT reference repos
> (see `Sources`). Open-source KB; company forks swap via the same slot
> (`README.md`).

## Principle

No one writes well in one pass. The first draft exists to get the ideas down;
revision is where clarity, structure, and tone actually get fixed. Treat the
first draft as disposable and the editing pass as the real work.

## Moves

- **Separate drafting from editing.** Get the whole thing down before you polish
  any of it. Editing while drafting stalls both.
- **Read it aloud.** Sentences you stumble over are sentences the reader will
  stumble over. This catches rhythm problems no checklist will.
- **Cut 10% on the second pass.** Almost every draft is too long. Delete the
  weakest sentence in each paragraph; the paragraph usually improves.
- **Check the opening last.** After the body is solid, you know what the piece is
  actually about — rewrite the first line to earn the read.
- **Run a final tell-sweep.** Before sending, scan for the AI tells: forced
  triads, em-dash overuse, "it's not just X, it's Y," sycophancy, hype words.
  See the pattern catalog in `_shared/patterns/`.

## Before / After

## When this matters most

Every piece that matters. The gap between a mediocre writer and a good one is
mostly the willingness to do a second and third pass.
